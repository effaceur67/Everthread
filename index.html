What is Fast Fashion?

Fast Fashion means producing clothes quickly and cheaply to follow the latest trends.
But behind every low price are polluted rivers, exploited workers, and mountains of textile waste.
The true cost of fashion is far greater than what we see on the price tag.

The Hidden Cost of Cheap Clothes

The fashion industry is one of the most polluting in the world.
It produces around 10% of global carbon emissions and uses thousands of liters of water for a single garment.
Tons of discared clothes end up in landfills or are burned, realising toxic chemicals into the air and soil.
Worse still, millions of children and workers are exploited in unsafe factories to keep prices low.

We Stand for Slow Fashion

Slow fashion is about quality, care, and respect - for the planet and for the people who make our clothes.
It means buying less but better: clothes that are well-made, ethically procuced, and designed to last.
We promote transparency, sustainibility, and creativity in every piece we offer

Our approach:

  • Support fair working conditions
  • Use natural and recycled materials
  • Encourage repair, reuse, and mindful consumption
